Barcelona

Sagrada Familia: Barcelona is home to the famous Sagrada Familia, a basilica designed by the renowned architect Antoni Gaudí.
Construction of the Sagrada Familia began in 1882 and continues to this day. It is expected to be completed in the next decade. The unique and intricate design of the basilica has made it one of Barcelona's most iconic landmarks. Gaudí's Influence: Antoni Gaudí, one of the most celebrated architects in the world, left a significant mark on Barcelona's architecture.
Besides the Sagrada Familia, Gaudí designed several other notable buildings, including Park Güell, Casa Batlló, and Casa Milà (La Pedrera). His distinctive modernist style, inspired by natural forms, has become synonymous with the city's artistic identity. La Rambla: La Rambla is a vibrant and famous boulevard in the heart of Barcelona. It stretches for about 1.2 kilometers,
connecting Plaça de Catalunya to the Christopher Columbus Monument at Port Vell. This bustling street is lined with shops, cafes, street performers, and market stalls. It's a popular spot for both locals and tourists to stroll and soak in the lively atmosphere.
