Valencia

City of Arts and Sciences: Valencia is home to the futuristic and architecturally stunning City of Arts and Sciences (Ciudad de las Artes y las Ciencias).
This complex includes several iconic structures designed by architect Santiago Calatrava, such as the Hemisférico (IMAX cinema and planetarium),
the Palau de les Arts Reina Sofia (opera house and performing arts center), and the Prince Felipe Science Museum. It has become a symbol of Valencia's commitment to modernity and innovation. La Tomatina Festival: Valencia hosts one of the world's most famous and unique festivals, La Tomatina. Held annually in the town of Buñol, just outside Valencia,
La Tomatina involves participants throwing overripe tomatoes at each other. It usually takes place in August and attracts thousands of people from around the world for this massive food fight. Valencian Paella: Paella, one of Spain's most famous dishes, originated in Valencia. Traditional Valencian paella typically includes rabbit, chicken, green beans, and snails,
seasoned with saffron and rosemary. While there are many variations of paella, the authentic Valencian version remains a culinary highlight of the region. Locals take pride in their paella, and enjoying this dish is a must when visiting Valencia.
